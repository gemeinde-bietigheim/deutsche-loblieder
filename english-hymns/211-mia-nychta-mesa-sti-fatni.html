211 MIA NYChTA MESA STI FATNI

Greek Hymns - No. 211 | "ΜΙΑ ΝΥΧΤΑ ΜΕΣΑ ΣΤΗ ΦΑΤΝΗ"
Verse 1:
Mia nychta mesa sti fatni egennithi
(One night in the manger he was born)
sti Vithleem ena theio paidi!
(in Bethlehem a divine child!)
Ti nychta ekeini ta ourania anoixan
(That night the heavens opened)
kai myrioi aggeloi psallan mazi!
(and myriads of angels sang together!)
Chorus:
Mega mystirio theias agapis,
(Great mystery of divine love,)
esteile o Plastis to mono Tou Yio,
(the Maker sent His only Son,)
aima na chysei, na mas lytrosei,
(blood to be shed, to redeem us,)
na mas ypsosei ston ourano!
(raise us to heaven!)
Verse 2:
Efanerothike eis tin partheno
(He appeared to the virgin)
archaggelos tou Theoy na tis pei:
(God's archangel to say to her:)
«Pneyma Kyriou tha s' episkiasei,
("Spirit of the Lord will overshadow you,)
gia na genniseis Christo Lytroti!».
(to give birth to Christ the Redeemer!".)
Verse 3:
Poimenes elavan mia aggelia,
(Shepherds received an announcement,)
oti Sotiras gennietai Christos!
(that Savior is born Christ!)
kai tha ton vroun gennimeno san vrefos
(and they shall find him born as a babe)
sparganomeno se fatni ftochon!
(broken in a manger of the poor!)
Verse 4:
Magoi sofoi xekinisan na erthoun
(Magical sages began to arrive)
sto Vasilia, gia na dosoun timi,
(to the King, to give honor,)
na tou prosferoun ta dora pou echoun,
(to offer him the gifts they have,)
na proskynisoune to Lytroti!
(to worship the Redeemer!)
Verse 5:
O Symeon ton perimene na 'rthei
(Simeon was waiting for him to come)
kai prosefchotane gia na Ton dei,
(and prayed to see Him,)
ki otan ton pire sta cheria tou eipe:
(and when he took him in his hands he said:)
«Eidan ta matia mou to Lytroti!».
("My eyes have seen the Redeemer!")
