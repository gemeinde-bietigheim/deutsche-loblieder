366 ITAN MIA MERA O IISOYS

Greek Hymns - No. 366 | "ΗΤΑΝ ΜΙΑ ΜΕΡΑ Ο ΙΗΣΟΥΣ"
Verse 1:
Itan mia mera o Iisoys
(There was one day Jesus)
stin akrogialia
(on the beach)
ki evlepe ekei dyo adelfoys
(and he saw two brothers there)
na psareyoune sta richa.
(to fish in the shallows.)
Richnan ta dichtya sta nera
(They were throwing the nets into the water)
amerimnoi aftoi,
(careless they)
otan akousan xafnika
(when they suddenly heard)
ton Iisoy Christo na tous kalei!
(may Jesus Christ call them!)
Chorus:
Afiste ta dichtya sas
(Let go of your nets)
kai piso mou elate,
(and come after me)
gia sas echo psarema
(I have fishing for you)
pou ta dika sas dichtya de choran!
(where your nets do not fit!)
Apo to chamo zoes
(From losing lives)
tha sozontai se ligo
(they will be saved in a moment)
kai anthropines psyches
(and human souls)
tha graftoyne sto Vivlio!
(they will be written in the Book!)
Verse 2:
Ta logia Tou san akousan,
(His words as they heard,)
tin idia stigmi
(at the same time)
ta dichtya tous paratisan
(their nets gave way)
me mia gnomi ki oi dyo mazi!
(with one opinion both together!)
Dichos kanena distagmo
(Without any hesitation)
ta logia tou akoysan
(his words heard)
kai tin aionia zoi
(and eternal life)
aftoi akolouthisan!
(they followed!)
