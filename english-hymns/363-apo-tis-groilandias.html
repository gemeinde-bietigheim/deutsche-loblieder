363 APO TIS GROILANDIAS

Greek Hymns - No. 363 | "ΑΠΟ ΤΗΣ ΓΡΟΙΛΑΝΔΙΑΣ"
Verse 1:
Apo tis Groilandias ta ori ta psychra,
(From Greenland the cold mountains,)
eos kai tis Indias ta dasi ta therma,
(up to India's warm forests,)
apantachoy stenazei plithos poly laon,
(everywhere groans multitudes of many peoples,)
na lysoume mas krazei tis planis ton desmon.
(to untie the bond of delusion.)
Verse 2:
Nisia aromatodi, koilades skieres,
(Fragrant islands, shadowy valleys,)
ployti tis gis mythodi, ektaseis thafmastes.
(riches of the land mythical, lands wondrous.)
Plin, an i fysi terpei tin orasi ekei,
(Except, if nature pleases the sight there,)
i anthropotis erpei, athlia kai tyfli.
(humanity creeps, wretched and blind.)
Verse 3:
Mataios promitheyei o Plastis agatha,
(In vain the Forger supplies goods,)
o anthropos latreyei agalmata, glypta,
(man worships statues, sculptures,)
me tyflomeno zilo latreyei os Theon,
(with blinded zeal he worships as God,)
to litho kai to xylo, ta erga ton cheiron.
(stone and wood, the works of hands.)
Verse 4:
O seis, oi fotisthentes me fos tou Ouranoy,
(O ye who are illumined by the light of Heaven,)
oi anagennithentes me logo tou Christoy,
(those born again by the word of Christ,)
kraxate: «Sotiria!», kiryxate pantoy
(you shouted: "Salvation!", you preached everywhere)
ti Chari tou Messia, Arniou tou Theoy!
(the Grace of the Messiah, Lamb of God!)
