81 METRAEI O VOSKOS TA PROVATA

Greek Hymns - No. 81 | "ΜΕΤΡΑΕΙ Ο ΒΟΣΚΟΣ ΤΑ ΠΡΟΒΑΤΑ"
Verse 1:
Metraei o voskos ta provata pou isan ekato
(The shepherd counts the sheep that were a hundred)
kai vriskei ena ap' afta pos leipei sto vouno.
(and finds one of them missing on the mountain.)
«Poy na 'nai arage» rota, «pou na planiet' ekei psila;»
("Where should he be," he asks, "where to planet up there?")
«poy na planiet' ekei psila;»
("where to soar up there?")
Verse 2:
Ti stani kleinei viastika ki afinei monacha
(He hastily closes the stall and leaves her alone)
ekei ta eneninta ennia kai trechei makria
(there the ninety-nine and runs away)
o storgikos kalos voskos, gia na vrei to apololos,
(the loving good shepherd, to find the lost,)
gia na vrei to apololos!
(to find the apololos!)
Verse 3:
Ma xafnika sti skoteinia charadres kai vouna
(But suddenly in the darkness ravines and mountains)
antilaloyn lypitera velasmata varia.
(mournful bleats moan heavily.)
Skirtaei o voskos apo chara sti gnorimi tou ti lalia,
(The shepherd squeals for joy at his acquaintance with the lily,)
sti gnorimi tou ti lalia!
(to his acquaintance the lalia!)
Verse 4:
Diavainei lofous, potamoys, charadres skoteines,
(He crosses hills, rivers, dark ravines,)
diavainei vrachous kai gkremoys, apotomes plagies,
(crosses rocks and cliffs, steep slopes,)
oyte stigmi de stamata kai tous kindynous apsifa,
(he does not stop for a moment and defies the dangers,)
kai tous kindynous apsifa!
(and he defies the dangers!)
Verse 5:
Ap' ti chara tou tin polli petaei san to pouli
(Out of his joy he flies like a bird)
kai sto vouno psila ekei, protoy charaxei i afgi,
(and on the mountain high there, ere the dawn breaks,)
to vriskei to apololos o storgikos kalos voskos,
(the loving good shepherd finds it alone,)
o storgikos kalos voskos!
(the loving good shepherd!)
Verse 6:
To agkaliazei, to filei, m' agapi storgiki
(He embraces it, kisses it, with affectionate love)
stous omous telos to kratei, sti stani t' odigei!
(finally he holds it on his shoulders, he leads it to the stable!)
«Chareite!», krazei o voskos, «to vrika to apololos»,
("Rejoice!" shouts the shepherd, "I have found it unspoiled",)
«to vrika to apololos!».
("I found it without fail!".)
