427 TO FORTIO TIS ZOIS

Greek Hymns - No. 427 | "ΤΟ ΦΟΡΤΙΟ ΤΗΣ ΖΩΗΣ"
Verse 1:
To fortio tis zois pou piezei tin psychi
(The burden of life that presses upon the soul)
den mporei na to sikosei monachos kaneis sti gi,
(no monk on earth can lift it,)
einai mayri i kardia kai i kathe symfora
(black is the heart and every calamity)
me lygizei kai mou spazei ta ftera.
(it bends me and breaks my wings.)
Chorus:
O Christe, mono Sy mporeis,
(O Christ, only You can,)
na mou pareis mes' ap' tin psychi
(take me through my soul)
to fortio kai tous fovous tis zois,
(the burden and fears of life,)
gia na eimai aionios asfalis!
(to be eternally safe!)
Verse 2:
San to provato ki ego pou 'chei pesei sto gkremo
(Like the sheep that has fallen over the cliff)
pligomenos kai chamenos mes ston kosmo trigyrno,
(wounded and lost in the world I roam,)
pouthena de vrisko fos, oyte fainetai voskos
(I find no light anywhere, nor is there a shepherd in sight)
na mou desei tis pliges na min pono.
(to bind my wounds so that I don't hurt.)
Verse 3:
San tyflos mes sti zoi pou skontaftei dystychis
(Like a blind man stumbling through life unhappily)
kai den xerei pou vadizei kai zitaei fos na dei,
(and he does not know where he walks and asks for light to see,)
etsi epsachna ki ego, ospou vrika to Christo
(so I searched, too, until I found Christ)
ki apo tote prochoro mazi m' Afton!
(and since then I have been moving forward with Him!)
